What Is Article Schema?

Article Schema is a Schema.org type that describes news articles, blog posts, and other written content. It provides search engines with essential metadata including headline, description, image, author, publication date, and article body. This information powers enhanced search features like the Top Stories carousel and article-rich results.

Google uses Article Schema to display your content in Google News, Google Discover, and the Top Stories section of search results. For publishers and content creators, this markup is essential for maximum visibility.

Common Mistakes

Missing or incorrect dates: Always use ISO 8601 date format (YYYY-MM-DD). Incorrect dates can prevent rich result eligibility.

Missing author information: Google prefers articles with clear author attribution for E-A-T signals.

Image without URL: The image field requires a full URL, not a relative path.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use Article Schema on every article page, not just the homepage
  • Include high-resolution images (at least 1200px wide) for Top Stories
  • Keep your headline under 110 characters for optimal display
  • Update the modified date when you update content
  • Combine with Breadcrumb Schema for comprehensive navigation markup

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Article Schema the same as BlogPosting Schema?

No. Article is the parent type. BlogPosting is a subtype used specifically for blog content. Use Article for news and general articles.

Do I need Article Schema for every post?

Yes, every article or blog post should have its own Article Schema markup for best search visibility.

Can I use Article Schema for videos?

No, use VideoObject Schema for video content. Article Schema is for written content.

How does Google use the article body field?

It helps Google understand the content for featured snippets and Google News categorization.

Will this help with Google Discover?

Yes, Article Schema is one of the signals Google uses for Discover content recommendations.
