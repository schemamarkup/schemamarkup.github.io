What Is Government Organization Schema?

Government Organization Schema is a subtype of Organization schema designed for governmental bodies including federal agencies, state departments, municipal offices, and public service organizations. It includes properties for official name, website, logo, description, address, and contact information. Google uses this markup to display government information in search results and knowledge panels, helping citizens access official services.

Frequently Asked Questions

What level of government can use this schema?

Government Organization Schema works for all levels: federal, state, county, and municipal government entities.

Can I use this for government-funded non-profits?

Use NGO schema for non-governmental organizations. Government Organization Schema is specifically for official government bodies.

Should I include all department contact details?

Include the main office contact information. Individual departments may benefit from their own schema on departmental pages.

Does this schema help with .gov site verification?

Schema markup doesn't replace domain-based verification, but it reinforces your official status in search results.

Can I mark up multiple government buildings?

Yes, create separate Government Organization schema blocks for each physical office location on their respective pages.
