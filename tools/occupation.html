What Is Occupation Schema?

Occupation Schema is a Schema.org type that describes a specific occupation or profession. It includes properties such as name, description, skills, qualifications, duties, education requirements, and salary information. This structured data helps search engines display detailed occupation profiles in rich results, giving job seekers comprehensive career information directly in search.

Google uses Occupation Schema to power enhanced job-related searches, displaying estimated salaries, required skills, and typical responsibilities. For employers and recruiters, this markup ensures your job postings reach the most qualified candidates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Occupation Schema the same as Job Posting Schema?

No. Occupation Schema describes the occupation itself, while JobPosting Schema describes a specific job opening. They complement each other.

Can I use Occupation Schema without salary data?

Yes, salary fields are optional. The schema is still valuable for skills and duties information.

What salary periods are supported?

Yearly, monthly, weekly, and hourly periods are all supported.

Does Occupation Schema help with Google for Jobs?

Indirectly. Use JobPosting Schema for Google for Jobs, but Occupation Schema provides enriched context.
