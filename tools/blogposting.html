What Is BlogPosting Schema?

BlogPosting Schema is a subtype of Article Schema designed specifically for blog content. It includes all Article properties plus additional support for keywords and blog-specific metadata. This structured data helps search engines distinguish blog posts from news articles and other content types.

Features

  • Blog-optimized schema with headline, description, and image
  • Author name and attribution for E-A-T signals
  • Keyword support for content categorization
  • Publication and modification date handling
  • Valid JSON-LD format ready to implement

Benefits

Rich Results for Blog Content

Blog posts with proper schema appear with images, dates, and author information in search results.

Better Content Discovery

Keywords in your schema markup help Google categorize and recommend your blog content to relevant audiences.

Author Authority

Clear author attribution builds expertise signals that Google values for ranking content.

How It Works

  1. Enter your blog post title and meta description.
  2. Add the featured image URL for rich snippet display.
  3. Set the publication date and optional modification date.
  4. Include the author name for attribution.
  5. Add your blog post URL and relevant keywords.
  6. Copy and paste the generated JSON-LD into your blog page.

When to Use It

Use BlogPosting Schema on every blog post, tutorial, case study, and listicle. It is specifically designed for the blog content format and signals to Google that your content is a blog post.

Use Cases

  • Personal and professional blogs
  • Company blogs and thought leadership content
  • Tutorial and how-to websites
  • Guest posts and contributed content
  • Listicles and curated content pages

Common Mistakes

Using Article Schema for blog posts: While Article works, BlogPosting is more specific and helps Google understand your content type.

Missing keywords: Keywords help Google categorize your content for relevant search queries.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Add BlogPosting Schema to every blog post individually
  • Use 3-5 relevant keywords that describe your post
  • Always include the author name for credibility
  • Update the modified date when you refresh content
  • Combine with Breadcrumb Schema for better site navigation understanding

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between BlogPosting and Article?

BlogPosting is a subtype of Article and is specifically for blog content. Article is broader and includes news articles.

Can I use BlogPosting for guest posts?

Yes. Include the guest author's name in the author field for proper attribution.

Do keywords in schema help rankings?

Keywords help Google understand your content topic, which can improve relevance matching.
