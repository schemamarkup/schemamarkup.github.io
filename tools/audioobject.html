What Is AudioObject Schema?

AudioObject Schema is a Schema.org type that describes audio content on the web. It includes properties like name, description, content URL, duration, author, and publication date. This structured data helps search engines index and display your audio content in rich results, making it discoverable to users searching for podcasts, audiobooks, or sound clips.

Google uses AudioObject markup to enhance search listings with playback duration, publication dates, and author information. It is the standard way to ensure your audio files are properly recognized and surfaced in search results.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ISO 8601 duration format?

Format durations as PT#H#M#S. For example, PT1H30M for 1 hour 30 minutes, or PT15M for 15 minutes.

Can I use AudioObject Schema for music files?

Yes, AudioObject works for any audio content. For music specifically, consider using MusicRecording Schema for richer music metadata.

Do I need to host the audio file myself?

No, the content URL can point to any hosted audio file, including third-party platforms.

Will AudioObject help with Google Podcasts?

Yes, Google Podcasts uses structured data from AudioObject and PodcastEpisode schema to index and display podcast content.

Is AudioObject Schema required for audio rich results?

While not strictly required, it significantly increases your chances of appearing in audio-rich search features.
