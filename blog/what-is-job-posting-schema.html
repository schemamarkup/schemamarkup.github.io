What is Job Posting Schema?

June 2026 · 551 words

JobPosting schema is a type of structured data designed for employment listings. It allows you to mark up job openings with detailed information including job title, description, salary, location, and employment type. When Google detects valid JobPosting schema, it can display your job listings in Google for Jobs, a dedicated job search feature that appears at the top of relevant search results. Google for Jobs is one of the most visible and valuable search features for recruiters and employers.

How JobPosting Schema Works

JobPosting schema uses the JobPosting type which includes properties for describing all aspects of a job opening. When implemented on your careers page or job listing page, Google can index your jobs and make them available in the Google for Jobs experience. Users searching for jobs on Google will see your listings alongside those from major job boards. The schema tells Google the job title, location, salary range, and how to apply.

Key Properties of JobPosting Schema

JobPosting schema includes essential properties for job listings. The title property is the job title. The description property provides a detailed job description. The hiringOrganization property identifies the employer using Organization schema. The jobLocation property specifies where the job is based using Place schema. The employmentType property indicates full-time, part-time, contract, or other arrangements. The baseSalary property provides salary information using MonetaryAmount or PriceSpecification schema. The datePosted indicates when the job was listed.

Required Properties for Google for Jobs

To be eligible for Google for Jobs, certain properties are required. The title, description, hiringOrganization, and jobLocation are required. You must also include either datePosted or an explicit validThrough date. The employmentType and baseSalary are strongly recommended. Google also requires that the description contains at least 50 characters. Meeting these requirements ensures your jobs are eligible for the widest possible distribution in Google for Jobs.

Salary and Compensation

The baseSalary property allows you to specify salary information in detail. Use MonetaryAmount schema with currency and value properties. You can specify an exact salary, a range using minValue and maxValue, or a starting salary. The salaryCurrency property uses ISO 4217 currency codes. The workHours property specifies expected hours per week. Transparent salary information helps attract qualified candidates and can improve application rates.

Job Location and Remote Work

The jobLocation property describes where the job is based. For remote jobs, use Place schema with a VirtualLocation or describe the remote arrangement in the description. The jobLocationType property can indicate remote, hybrid, or on-site work arrangements. For jobs with multiple locations, include multiple jobLocation entries. For travel-intensive roles, describe the geographic area using the applicableLocation property. Accurate location information helps job seekers find relevant positions.

Hiring Organization Details

The hiringOrganization property uses Organization schema to describe the employer. Include the organization name, url, and logo. A complete organization profile helps job seekers learn about your company and can improve application rates. The sameAs property can link to your company's LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and other employer review profiles. A strong employer brand represented in schema can differentiate your job listings from competitors.

Conclusion

JobPosting schema is essential for any organization posting jobs online. It enables your listings to appear in Google for Jobs, reaching a vast audience of active job seekers.
