Schema Markup for Local SEO

June 2026 · 538 words

Schema markup is one of the most effective tools for local SEO. It helps search engines understand your business location, hours, services, and contact information. Local Business schema directly impacts your visibility in local search results, the local pack, and Google Maps. This guide covers the essential schema types for local businesses and how to implement them for maximum local search impact.

Local Business Schema

Local Business schema is the foundation of local SEO markup. It extends Organization with location-specific properties including address, telephone, opening hours, and geographic coordinates. Local Business schema helps your business appear in the local pack, the map-based results that appear for location-specific searches. It also powers your Google Business Profile and ensures consistency across Google surfaces.

Complete Your NAP Information

NAP stands for Name, Address, and Phone number. Your Local Business schema must include consistent NAP information that matches your Google Business Profile and other online listings. Use the name, address (using PostalAddress schema), and telephone properties. Inconsistencies in NAP information across the web can harm your local search rankings. Use the exact same business name, address format, and phone number everywhere.

Business Hours

The openingHoursSpecification property describes when your business is open. Use OpeningHoursSpecification schema to specify regular hours for each day of the week. You can also specify holiday hours and special closures. Accurate hours information helps customers know when to visit and can be displayed directly in search results. Google may also indicate whether your business is currently open or closed based on this data.

Geographic Coordinates

The geo property using GeoCoordinates schema provides the latitude and longitude of your business location. This information is essential for Google Maps placement and location-based search features. Use a reliable tool to find your exact coordinates. Even small errors in coordinates can misplace your business on the map. Accurate coordinates ensure that customers can find your physical location easily.

Local Business Subtypes

Use the most specific Local Business subtype for your industry. Restaurant, Store, DentalClinic, HairSalon, and hundreds of other subtypes exist. Each subtype may enable industry-specific features in search results. For example, Restaurant schema can enable menu and reservation features, while Dentist schema can enable appointment scheduling. Using the correct subtype maximizes your rich result opportunities.

Reviews and Ratings

AggregateRating schema displays average star ratings in local search results. Positive reviews with high ratings can significantly improve click-through rates for local businesses. The review property using Review schema marks up individual customer reviews. Google has strict guidelines for review markup to prevent manipulation. Ensure all reviews are genuine and visible on your page.

Service and Price Range

The priceRange property indicates the cost level of your business using a simple notation like $, $$, or $$$. For service-based businesses, the areaServed property describes the geographic area you serve. The makesOffer property can describe specific services using Offer schema. These properties help customers understand your pricing and service area before they visit or contact you.

Conclusion

Local Business schema is essential for any business with a physical location. It improves local search visibility, powers Google Maps listings, and helps customers find and choose your business.
