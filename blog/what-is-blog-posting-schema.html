What is Blog Posting Schema?

June 2026 · 557 words

BlogPosting schema is a subtype of Article schema specifically designed for blog posts. It inherits all properties from Article and CreativeWork while adding properties specific to blog content. BlogPosting schema helps search engines understand that your content is a blog post and display it appropriately in search results. For bloggers and content marketers, BlogPosting schema is an essential tool for improving search visibility.

Why BlogPosting Schema Matters

BlogPosting schema matters because it tells search engines specifically that your content is a blog post rather than a news article, technical document, or other type of content. This distinction matters because blog posts may be treated differently in search results than news articles. BlogPosting schema also enables rich results that display your blog post's headline, featured image, author, and publication date directly in search listings, making them more attractive and informative to users.

Key Properties

BlogPosting schema includes all the properties of Article schema plus a few additional ones. The headline property contains your blog post title. The author property identifies the writer. The datePublished and dateModified properties track publication and update dates. The image property provides the featured image. The articleBody contains the full post content. BlogPosting also inherits the speakable property for voice search optimization and the mainEntityOfPage property to identify the primary topic of your post.

BlogPosting vs. Article Schema

While BlogPosting is a subtype of Article, there are practical differences in how they should be used. Use BlogPosting for content published on a blog, especially if it is part of a regular series of posts. Use Article for standalone articles and news content. Use NewsArticle for time-sensitive news content. The choice of schema type helps search engines understand the nature of your content and apply the appropriate treatment in search results.

Categories and Tags

BlogPosting schema supports the about and keywords properties for categorizing your content. The about property identifies the main subject of your post using Thing schema. The keywords property accepts a list of relevant keywords or tags. These properties help search engines understand the topics your blog post covers and match it to relevant search queries. Use specific, relevant categories and keywords that accurately reflect your content.

Related Posts and Navigation

BlogPosting schema can be combined with other schema types to improve navigation and content discovery. The isPartOf property can link your post to a Blog schema that represents your entire blog. The hasPart property can link to related posts or content series. These relationships help search engines understand how your content is organized and can improve the navigation experience for users.

Comments and Interaction

If your blog supports comments, you can use the comment property to mark up individual comments using Comment schema. Each comment can include the author, text, and date. Marking up comments helps search engines understand the community engagement around your content and may enable discussion-related features in search results. However, only mark up genuine, moderated comments to avoid spam issues.

Conclusion

BlogPosting schema is essential for bloggers who want their content to stand out in search results. It enables rich results, helps search engines understand your content type, and supports voice search optimization.
