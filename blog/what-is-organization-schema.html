What is Organization Schema?

June 2026 · 658 words

Organization schema is a type of structured data that allows you to provide search engines with detailed information about your business or organization. It is one of the most fundamental and widely used schema types. Organization schema helps search engines understand who you are, what your business does, and how to contact you. It is the foundation for appearing in knowledge panels and is recommended for virtually every website.

Key Properties of Organization Schema

Organization schema includes a comprehensive set of properties to describe your business. The name property is required and should contain your official business name. The url property specifies your website address. The logo property points to your organization's logo image. The contactPoint property allows you to specify customer service contact information including phone number and email. The sameAs property links to your social media profiles on platforms like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Instagram.

Organization Schema and Knowledge Panels

One of the most valuable benefits of Organization schema is its role in generating knowledge panels. Knowledge panels are the information boxes that appear on the right side of Google search results when someone searches for your business. They display key information like your business name, logo, description, and social media links. Google uses Organization schema from your website along with other authoritative sources to build these knowledge panels. Implementing Organization schema increases the likelihood that Google will create an accurate and complete knowledge panel for your business.

Required and Recommended Properties

While Organization schema has relatively few required properties for basic validation, certain properties are recommended for the best results. The name property is the only strictly required property. However, for knowledge panel eligibility, you should include url, logo, and sameAs. For local search visibility, include address and contactPoint. For brand recognition, include the legalName and alternateName properties. The more complete your markup, the better search engines can understand and display your organization.

Nested Objects in Organization Schema

Organization schema supports several nested objects that provide additional detail. The address property uses PostalAddress schema to describe your physical address with properties like streetAddress, addressLocality, addressRegion, and postalCode. The contactPoint property uses ContactPoint schema to specify phone numbers, email addresses, and contact types. The location property uses Place schema to describe your geographic location with geo coordinates. These nested objects allow you to provide rich, detailed information about your organization.

Multiple Organization Markup

If your business has multiple locations or departments, you can use multiple Organization schema instances on your website. Each location or department can have its own Organization markup with specific name, address, and contact information. For parent-child relationships between organizations, use the parentOrganization and subOrganization properties. This allows search engines to understand the relationship between your main business and its branches or subsidiaries.

Organization Schema for Different Business Types

Organization schema is the base type for many specialized business schemas. LocalBusiness, Corporation, EducationalOrganization, GovernmentOrganization, MedicalOrganization, NGO, PerformingGroup, and SportsOrganization are all subtypes of Organization. Each subtype inherits all Organization properties and adds specialized properties for that industry. If your business falls into one of these categories, use the more specific subtype for better search engine understanding.

Implementation Best Practices

When implementing Organization schema, follow these best practices. Use your official business name exactly as it appears on legal documents. Ensure your logo image meets Google's size and aspect ratio requirements for knowledge panels. Include all your active social media profiles in the sameAs property. Keep your markup updated when your business information changes. Test your markup using Google's Rich Results Test to ensure it is valid and complete.

Conclusion

Organization schema is essential for any business website. It helps search engines understand who you are, enables knowledge panels, and provides the foundation for other schema types.
