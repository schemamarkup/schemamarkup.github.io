What is the Google Knowledge Graph?

June 2026 · 471 words

The Google Knowledge Graph is a knowledge base that Google uses to understand entities people, places, things and their relationships. It powers knowledge panels, the information boxes that appear on the right side of search results for notable entities. Schema markup is one of the signals Google uses to build and verify the Knowledge Graph. Understanding the Knowledge Graph helps you create schema markup that improves your entity visibility in Google.

How the Knowledge Graph Works

The Knowledge Graph connects entities through relationships. For example, it connects a person to their employer, a book to its author, and a movie to its actors. These connections are built from multiple sources including schema markup, Wikipedia, Wikidata, and other authoritative data sources. When Google processes schema markup on your website, it extracts entity information and relationship data to add to the Knowledge Graph. The more complete and accurate your schema markup, the better Google can understand your entity.

Knowledge Panels

Knowledge panels are the most visible manifestation of the Knowledge Graph. They display key information about an entity including name, description, logo, social media links, and key facts. Knowledge panels appear for organizations, people, places, movies, books, and many other entity types. Schema markup, particularly Organization schema and Person schema, helps Google gather the information needed to create and populate knowledge panels for your business or personal brand.

Entity Recognition and Disambiguation

The Knowledge Graph helps Google recognize entities and distinguish between entities with similar names. For example, Apple the fruit vs. Apple the company. Schema markup helps with entity disambiguation by providing clear type information and unique identifiers. The sameAs property links your schema markup to other authoritative sources like Wikipedia and Wikidata, helping Google confirm your entity's identity and distinguish it from similar entities.

Schema Types and the Knowledge Graph

Different schema types contribute to different aspects of the Knowledge Graph. Organization schema helps build business and brand entities. Person schema builds individual profiles. Place schema builds location entities. CreativeWork schema builds content entities like articles, books, and movies. The more schema types you use correctly, the more entities Google can recognize and connect on your behalf.

SameAs and External References

The sameAs property is one of the most important for Knowledge Graph integration. It provides links to other authoritative sources that confirm your entity's identity. Include sameAs links to your Wikipedia page, Wikidata entry, social media profiles, and other official online presences. These cross-references help Google verify your entity and build a more complete Knowledge Graph profile with accurate connections to related entities.

Conclusion

The Google Knowledge Graph is fundamental to how Google understands entities and displays information in search results. Schema markup helps Google build and verify your entity profile.
