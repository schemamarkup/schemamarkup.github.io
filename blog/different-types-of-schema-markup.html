What are the different types of Schema Markup?

June 2026 · 503 words

Schema.org defines hundreds of types of schema markup organized into categories based on the kind of content they describe. Understanding the different types helps you choose the right one for your content.

Business and Organization Types

These schema types describe organizations, businesses, and their attributes. Organization schema is the most fundamental type, providing properties for name, logo, contact information, and social media profiles. LocalBusiness extends Organization with location-specific properties like address, phone number, and hours of operation. Subtypes include Restaurant, Store, and MedicalBusiness for specialized business types.

Content and Creative Work Types

CreativeWork schema covers a broad range of content types including Article, BlogPosting, NewsArticle, Book, Movie, MusicRecording, and more. Each type has properties specific to that content format. Article schema includes properties for headline, author, publication date, and article body. Recipe schema adds properties for cooking time, ingredients, and nutritional information.

Ecommerce Types

Product schema is the primary type for ecommerce, with properties for name, description, brand, offers, and reviews. Offer schema describes pricing and availability for products and services. AggregateRating schema provides overall ratings from multiple reviews. ShippingDeliveryTime and MerchantReturnPolicy schemas support detailed shipping and returns information.

Event Types

Event schema describes organized events with properties for date, time, location, and ticket information. Subtypes include BusinessEvent, ChildrensEvent, ComedyEvent, DanceEvent, EducationEvent, Festival, FoodEvent, MusicEvent, SaleEvent, SocialEvent, SportsEvent, and TheaterEvent. Each subtype inherits all Event properties while adding specialized characteristics.

Local and Place Types

Place schema describes physical locations with properties for address, geo coordinates, and photos. LocalBusiness subtypes like Restaurant, Hotel, and Hospital extend Place with industry-specific properties. TouristAttraction, Landmark, and Park schemas describe points of interest for travel and tourism content.

Review and Rating Types

Review schema describes a single review with properties for review body, rating, and reviewer. AggregateRating schema provides summary statistics like average rating and review count from multiple reviews. Rating schema represents a numeric or star rating that can be used within Review or independently.

Media Types

VideoObject, AudioObject, and ImageObject schemas describe media content with properties for duration, thumbnail, upload date, and transcript. MusicRecording, MusicAlbum, and MusicGroup schemas describe musical content. Movie and TVSeries schemas describe film and television content with properties for cast, director, and rating.

Health and Medical Types

Schema.org includes extensive medical types under the MedicalEntity category. These cover conditions, treatments, drugs, medical tests, and healthcare procedures. MedicalCondition, Drug, MedicalProcedure, and MedicalTest are some of the most commonly used medical schema types.

Action and Interaction Types

Action schema describes actions that users can take, such as SearchAction, BuyAction, and ListenAction. These are often used in combination with other schema types to enable interactive features in search results. For example, SitelinksSearchBox uses SearchAction to enable on-site search directly from search results.

With so many types available, it's important to choose the one that most accurately describes your content. Using the correct type ensures that search engines understand your content properly and can display it in the most appropriate rich result format.
