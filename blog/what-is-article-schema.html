What is Article Schema?

June 2026 · 625 words

Article schema is a type of structured data for marking up news articles, blog posts, and other written content. It belongs to the CreativeWork category in Schema.org and provides properties specifically designed for articles. Article schema helps search engines understand the key elements of your article including the headline, author, publication date, and featured image. It is essential for appearing in Google News, Top Stories, and other news-related search features.

Key Properties of Article Schema

Article schema includes several important properties. The headline property contains the title of your article. The author property identifies who wrote the article using Person schema. The datePublished and dateModified properties indicate when the article was published and last updated. The image property provides the article's featured image. The articleBody property contains the full text of the article. The description property provides a summary for search result snippets.

Types of Article Schema

Schema.org defines several subtypes of Article for different content formats. Article is the base type for all articles. NewsArticle is for news content and is required for Google News inclusion. BlogPosting is for blog posts. TechArticle is for technical and instructional content. ScholarlyArticle is for academic papers. SocialMediaPosting is for content published on social media platforms. Using the correct subtype ensures that search engines understand the nature of your content and apply the appropriate treatment.

Article Schema for Google News

NewsArticle schema is required for appearing in Google News and the Top Stories carousel in search results. Google News uses the schema to understand the headline, publication date, and source of news articles. Articles with valid NewsArticle markup are eligible for inclusion in Google News, which can drive significant traffic. The schema also helps Google distinguish between breaking news, analysis, and opinion pieces, allowing for appropriate categorization.

Speaking and Pronunciation Properties

Article schema supports properties for enhancing the presentation of your content in search results. The speakable property identifies sections of your article that are suitable for text-to-speech conversion, making them available for Google Assistant and other voice services. The pronunciation property helps Google pronounce unusual words or names correctly. These properties are particularly valuable for news publishers who want their content to be accessible through voice interfaces.

Author and Publisher Information

Proper author and publisher markup is important for credibility and attribution. The author property should use Person schema to identify the writer with properties for name, url, and sameAs for social media profiles. The publisher property should use Organization schema to identify the publishing entity with logo and name. Google uses this information to display bylines and publisher information in search results, which helps users assess the credibility of your content.

Time-Sensitive Content

Article schema includes properties for time-sensitive content. The datePublished property tells Google when your article was first published. The dateModified property indicates when it was last updated. Google uses these dates to ensure freshness in news results and to avoid displaying outdated content. For evergreen content that is periodically updated, always update the dateModified property to reflect the latest revision.

Implementation Tips

When implementing Article schema, ensure your headline matches the visible headline on your page. Use absolute URLs for all links including author profiles and publisher logos. Include a high-quality image that meets Google's requirements for rich results. Set the datePublished property in ISO 8601 format. Test your markup using Google's Rich Results Test to confirm it is valid and eligible for article rich results.

Conclusion

Article schema is essential for news publishers, bloggers, and content creators. It enables your content to appear in Google News, Top Stories, and rich search results with headline, image, and author information.
