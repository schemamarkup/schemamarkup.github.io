What is Local Business Schema?

June 2026 · 635 words

Local Business schema is a specialized type of schema markup designed for businesses that serve customers at a physical location. It extends Organization schema with properties specific to local businesses, including address, phone number, hours of operation, and geographic coordinates. Local Business schema is essential for appearing in local search results, the local pack, and Google Maps. It helps potential customers find your business and get the information they need to visit or contact you.

Key Properties of Local Business Schema

Local Business schema includes all the properties of Organization schema plus additional properties for location-specific information. The address property using PostalAddress schema is crucial for local search. The telephone property provides your phone number. The openingHoursSpecification property describes your business hours for each day of the week. The geo property provides latitude and longitude coordinates for Google Maps. The priceRange property indicates the cost level of your business. These properties together provide a complete picture of your local business.

Local Business Schema and Local Search

Local Business schema directly impacts your visibility in local search results. When Google understands your business location, hours, and contact information, it can display your business in the local pack — the map-based results that appear for location-specific queries. Local Business schema also powers your Google Business Profile, ensuring that the information displayed is accurate and consistent with your website. This consistency is a ranking signal for local search.

Subtypes of Local Business

Local Business has many subtypes for specific industries. Restaurant, Store, HealthClub, HairSalon, Dentist, Doctor, VeterinaryCare, TravelAgency, RealEstateAgent, and hundreds more. Each subtype inherits all Local Business properties and may add industry-specific properties. Using the correct subtype helps Google understand your specific type of business and can enable industry-specific rich results. For example, Restaurant schema can enable food-related features, while Dentist schema can enable healthcare-related features.

Hours of Operation

The openingHoursSpecification property is critical for local businesses. It uses OpeningHoursSpecification schema to specify the days and times your business is open. You can specify regular hours, holiday hours, and special closures. Google uses this information to show your business hours in search results and to indicate whether your business is currently open. Accurate hours information helps potential customers know when to visit and reduces the likelihood of negative experiences.

Multiple Locations

If your business has multiple locations, create separate Local Business schema for each location. Each location should have its own page or section on your website with unique address, phone number, and hours. Use the parentOrganization property to link each location to your main organization. This approach ensures that each location can appear in local search results independently while maintaining the relationship to your overall business.

Integration with Google Business Profile

Local Business schema works together with your Google Business Profile to provide consistent information across Google surfaces. The information in your schema markup should match the information in your Google Business Profile exactly. Any inconsistencies can confuse Google and may harm your local search visibility. Regularly audit both your schema markup and your Business Profile to ensure consistency in business name, address, phone number, hours, and categories.

Reviews and Ratings

Local Business schema can include review and rating information using the aggregateRating and review properties. These properties display customer satisfaction information in search results and can significantly influence click-through rates. Positive reviews with high ratings make your listing more attractive to potential customers. Ensure that your review markup complies with Google's guidelines, which require that reviews are genuine and verifiable.

Conclusion

It improves visibility in local search, powers Google Maps listings, and provides customers with the information they need.
